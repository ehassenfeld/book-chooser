About the Audiobook Chooser

← Back to audiobooks

A tool for browsing kids' audiobooks available right now at the Berkeley Public Library (via Libby). It draws from a dataset of ~220 middle-grade audiobooks suited to listeners roughly ages 8–12, checks which ones are currently available, and shows the top 20 as a browsable grid of covers.

How It Works

When the page loads, it ranks the full list of audiobooks using a weighted score (favoring age-appropriate, engaging, and shorter titles), then checks the Berkeley Public Library's digital catalog via the OverDrive API. As available audiobooks are found, they appear on screen. The page stops once it has 20 available titles to show.

Each card shows the book's cover art (from the library catalog), a brief description of the story, and tags for genre, listening length, series info, and target age range. The "Sample" button opens the book's page on the library's OverDrive site, where you can listen to a preview and borrow the audiobook.

Ranking

Books are ranked by a composite score across four dimensions. The ranking determines which available audiobooks appear first — the best matches show up at the top of the grid.

Age Fit (weight: 70) How well the audiobook's target age range matches a 7–8 year old listener. Books whose range includes ages 7–8 score highest.
Engagement (weight: 70) How gripping the audiobook is — pacing, hooks, cliffhangers. A 1–5 scale where 5 means "can't stop listening" (Percy Jackson, Wings of Fire) and 1 means contemplative or literary pacing.
Length (weight: 50) Favors shorter audiobooks on a log scale. Shorter titles are easier for younger listeners building stamina.
Recency (weight: 30) Slight preference for more recently published books, on a log scale.

Genres

Fantasy Magic, mythical creatures, other worlds. Harry Potter, Narnia, Percy Jackson.
Adventure Survival, quests, exploration. Hatchet, Treasure Island, Holes.
Mystery Puzzles, investigations, secrets. The Mysterious Benedict Society, The Westing Game, Enola Holmes.
Realistic Fiction Contemporary stories grounded in the real world. Ramona, Wonder, Front Desk.
Historical Fiction Stories set in a specific historical period. Number the Stars, Esperanza Rising, Refugee.
Humor Comedy is the primary mode. Diary of a Wimpy Kid, Captain Underpants, Sideways Stories from Wayside School.
Sci-Fi Future technology, space, time travel. A Wrinkle in Time, The Giver, The Last Kids on Earth.
Animal Stories Animals as central characters. Charlotte's Web, Warriors, The One and Only Ivan.

Data and Limitations

The dataset of ~220 books and all metadata (descriptions, engagement scores, age ranges, listening hours) were generated by Claude (Anthropic). There are likely errors — listening hours and narrator credits in particular should be taken as rough estimates. Availability is checked live against the Berkeley Public Library's OverDrive catalog each time the page loads.
